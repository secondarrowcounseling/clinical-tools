Mapping Your Daily Demands

Autistic burnout occurs when long-term everyday demands consistently exceed your system's energy reserves. Because everyone's environment is unique, this page uses simple scales to help you estimate the general pressure inside different areas of your life. Adjust the sliders to reflect your current load, and optionally type in any specific factors you want to anchor for our conversation.

Sensory Demands

0
Think about ambient background environments: fluorescent light grids, unpredictable community noise, temperature variations, or persistent clothing texture friction.

Social & Interaction Demands

0
Think about communication rules: interpreting ambiguous text channels, managing small talk loops, or predicting unwritten social performance standards.

Executive & Logistic Demands

0
Think about everyday organization: managing intricate forms or administrative files, task sequencing friction, or dealing with sudden interruptions mid-task.

Masking & Compensation

0
Think about intentional pacing: manually simulating standard expressions or vocal tones, suppressing physical grounding movements, or enduring discomfort silently.

Routine & Transition Disruption

0
Think about unexpected adjustments: sudden schedule edits, unscheduled transit variations, or stepping into unmapped roles or spaces.

Internal Bodily Stress

0
Think about physiological updates: muted or delayed hunger signals, managing chronic health vulnerability, or processing systemic hormonal fluctuations.

Understanding the Mechanism

The Alternator vs. The Battery

A typical nervous system often operates like a car alternator—engaging in low-stakes social connection or everyday tasks doesn't completely drain them; it can sometimes act to recharge or sustain general arousal.

An ADHD, Autistic, or AuDHD brain operates almost entirely on battery. Social interactions, sensory environments, and task switches draw heavily on finite metabolic reserves. This drain occurs silently and continuously.

Why Silence is Not Rejection

When reserves hit critical zones, the brain automatically initiates Power-Saving Mode. It suppresses high-bandwidth processes (such as speech processing, facial animation, and predictive empathy loops).

This is a survival function, not an emotional statement. By understanding this state, you can support your partner by helping protect their isolation space, which actively restores their energy so they can safely re-engage sooner.

What is autistic burnout?

Autistic burnout is not ordinary exhaustion. It is not clinical depression, though it frequently overlaps with both and is often misidentified. It is a predictable biological response to cumulative, long-term friction. It reflects the high operational cost of running a sensitive nervous system in an environment configured for a completely different neurotype.

The experience is characterized by three core markers: deep processing depletion, a temporary shift in executive or verbal capacity, and a drastically lowered threshold for raw ambient data and social scripts. It is a long-term adjustment state, meaning it requires systematic restructuring rather than a simple weekend pause to resolve.

The Master Channel Distinction

In Autistic Burnout: Your core special interests and deep passions still feel vitalizing and magnetic. They are currently behind a thick pane of glass because of energy depletion, but the pull towards them remains alive. You can clearly imagine the delight of tracking them if your mixer had the necessary power capital.

In Mood Depression: The channel itself goes flat or silent. The focus interest loses its pull entirely, and motivation is altered at a chemical level, independent of your actual energetic battery charge.

If your deep interest feels like yours but is locked away behind a wall of pure physical exhaustion, that points to burnout as the configuration driving your system.
